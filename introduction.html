What is Chess?

Chess is a two-player strategy game that is played on an 8x8 board. The game is played with 16 pieces for each player, including a king, queen, rooks, knights, bishops, and pawns. The objective of the game is to checkmate the opponent's king, which means putting the king in a position where it cannot escape capture.

How to Play Chess

Chess is a game of strategy and tactics. Each piece has its own unique movement pattern, and players must use their pieces to control the board and attack the opponent's pieces. The game is won by putting the opponent's king in checkmate, which means the king is in a position to be captured and cannot escape. For more information please refer to the video below.

Chess Pieces and Moves

Piece Move
King One square in any direction
Queen Any number of squares in any direction
Rook Any number of squares horizontally or vertically
Bishop Any number of squares diagonally
Knight Move in an L-shape: two squares in one direction and then one square in a perpendicular direction
Pawn Move forward one square, capture diagonally

Chess Notation

Chess notation is a system for recording moves in a chess game. Each square on the board is identified by a letter and a number, and each piece is represented by a letter. For example, the move e4 means moving the pawn to the square e4.

Chess Openings

Here you will find some of the most common chess openings

Opening Description
Italian Game This is a super classic opening where White develops their pieces quickly, aiming for control over the center. It's known for being very tactical.
Sicilian Defense This is a super popular response to 1.e4. Black immediately challenges the center, and it can lead to very sharp and tactical positions.
French Defense A solid opening where Black prepares to challenge White's center later on with d5. It can lead to closed, strategic positions.
Queen's Gambit A super popular and classic opening where White offers a pawn to gain control of the center. It’s a great mix of strategy and tactics. Fun Fact You may have heard of a show named this, this is the opening it is based on.
King's Indian Defense Black aims to fianchetto their bishop and build up pressure against White’s center. It’s a very dynamic opening.

These are just some of the foundational openings to get you started. As you play more and gain experience, you'll discover a whole world of other strategies and variations to explore. The deeper you go, the more you'll learn and refine your game!
